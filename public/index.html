How It Works

Our allocation system uses a set of transparent rules to determine job eligibility. Here's what each rule means in plain English.

⚙

Rule-Based Allocation Engine

Every time a student is considered for a job, the system runs through a series of checks automatically. If any rule is violated, the allocation is blocked and the reason is clearly explained. This ensures fairness, prevents overloading companies and jobs, and keeps the process transparent.

🎯

Fit Score Threshold

FIT_THRESHOLD

A student must have a fit score of 70 or above to be considered for any job allocation. This ensures only well-matched candidates are placed, improving outcomes for both students and employers.

Example: A student with a fit score of 65 will be blocked from allocation until their score improves.
📋

Job Weekly Cap

JOB_CAP

Each job posting can receive a maximum of 12 allocations per week. Once a job reaches this limit, no further students can be allocated to it until the next week begins.

Example: A popular job that has already received 12 applications this week will be temporarily unavailable.
🏢

Company Weekly Cap

COMPANY_CAP

Each company can receive a maximum of 30 total allocations per week across all their job postings. This prevents any single company from being overwhelmed with applications.

Example: A company with 30 allocations this week will not receive any more until next week resets.
👤

Student Weekly Cap

STUDENT_WEEKLY_CAP

Each student can be allocated to a maximum of 5 jobs per week. This encourages thoughtful job applications and prevents students from mass-applying without consideration.

Example: A student who has already been allocated 5 jobs this week must wait until next Monday.
⌛

Cooldown Period

COOLDOWN

After being allocated to or rejected from a specific job, a student must wait through a cooldown period before reapplying to the same job. This prevents spamming and gives other students a fair chance.

Example: A student who recently applied to "Frontend Engineer at Acme" must wait before applying again.
🔄

How Checks Are Ordered

FLOW

Rules are checked in a specific order. The first failing rule stops the process and returns a clear reason code.

  1. Job exists and company matches
  2. Fit score meets the threshold (≥ 70)
  3. Job hasn't hit its weekly cap (12)
  4. Company hasn't hit its weekly cap (30)
  5. Student hasn't hit their weekly cap (5)
  6. Student is not in a cooldown period for that job
